---

## 1. Purpose

This SOP defines the vibration inspection procedure for P-101 Cooling Water Circulation Pump.

The purpose of this procedure is to detect abnormal vibration early, identify possible bearing or alignment issues, and prevent unplanned pump failure.

---

## 2. Scope

This SOP applies only to:

Asset ID: P-101
Asset Name: Cooling Water Circulation Pump
Asset Type: Centrifugal Pump
Location: Utility Area - Line A

This SOP does not apply to C-201 Compressor or HX-301 Heat Exchanger.

---

## 3. Vibration Thresholds

| Condition |    Vibration Reading |
| --------- | -------------------: |
| Normal    |       Below 4.5 mm/s |
| Warning   | 4.5 mm/s to 7.5 mm/s |
| Critical  |       Above 7.5 mm/s |

If vibration exceeds 7.5 mm/s, the issue must be escalated immediately.

---

## 4. Inspection Frequency

P-101 vibration inspection must be performed as part of routine condition monitoring.

Minimum inspection frequency:

Weekly vibration check.

Additional inspection is required when abnormal bearing noise, high bearing temperature, or unusual pump behavior is reported.

---

## 5. Inspection Procedure

1. Verify that the pump is operating under normal load.
2. Collect vibration reading near the bearing housing.
3. Compare the reading against vibration thresholds.
4. Record vibration value in the inspection report.
5. Check for abnormal bearing noise.
6. Check for visible looseness or mounting issues.
7. Review lubrication evidence.
8. Escalate if vibration exceeds warning or critical limit.

---

## 6. Required Evidence

Every vibration inspection must record:

* Asset ID
* Inspection date
* Technician name
* Vibration reading
* Operating condition
* Bearing noise observation
* Recommended action
* Linked work order if abnormal

---

## 7. Abnormal Condition Criteria

P-101 should be treated as abnormal if any of the following are found:

* Vibration reading is above 6.0 mm/s.
* Vibration reading is above 7.5 mm/s.
* Abnormal bearing noise is observed.
* Bearing temperature is close to 80°C.
* Lubrication evidence is missing.
* Shaft misalignment is suspected.

---

## 8. Recommended Action

If vibration is above warning threshold:

1. Continue monitoring.
2. Check bearing noise.
3. Verify lubrication evidence.
4. Check alignment during next maintenance window.

If vibration is above critical threshold:

1. Create urgent maintenance work order.
2. Inspect bearing housing.
3. Verify lubrication level and lubricant quality.
4. Check shaft alignment.
5. Inspect mounting bolts.
6. Review incident history.
