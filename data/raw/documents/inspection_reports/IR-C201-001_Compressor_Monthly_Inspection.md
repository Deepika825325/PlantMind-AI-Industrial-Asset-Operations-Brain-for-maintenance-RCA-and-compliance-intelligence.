---

## 1. Inspection Purpose

This inspection was performed as part of the monthly condition review for C-201 Process Air Compressor.

The inspection focused on compressor operating behavior, outlet temperature, pressure ratio stability, filter replacement verification, and maintenance planning.

---

## 2. Inspection Scope

The inspection covered:

* Outlet temperature trend
* Pressure ratio stability
* Estimated Remaining Useful Life trend
* Filter replacement verification
* Maintenance planning requirement
* Safe shutdown procedure reference

---

## 3. Measurement Summary

| Parameter          | Measured Value |    Normal Range |   Warning Limit |  Critical Limit | Status  |
| ------------------ | -------------: | --------------: | --------------: | --------------: | ------- |
| Outlet temperature |           91°C |    70°C to 85°C |      Above 90°C |      Above 95°C | Warning |
| Pressure ratio     |           3.95 |      3.2 to 3.8 |       Above 3.9 |       Above 4.1 | Warning |
| Estimated RUL      |      36 cycles | Above 50 cycles | Below 40 cycles | Below 25 cycles | Warning |

---

## 4. Findings

* Outlet temperature increased to 91°C.
* Normal outlet temperature range is 70°C to 85°C.
* Pressure ratio instability was observed during loaded operation.
* Estimated Remaining Useful Life trend is decreasing.
* Filter replacement was planned, but verification evidence is delayed.
* Compressor inspection should be planned during the next maintenance window.

---

## 5. Risk Interpretation

C-201 is considered medium risk because multiple warning-level indicators are present.

The current condition may indicate:

* Compressor degradation
* Filter restriction
* Increased operating load
* Pressure instability
* Need for planned maintenance

There is no immediate critical failure evidence, but maintenance planning is required.

---

## 6. Recommended Action

The maintenance team should:

1. Verify filter replacement completion.
2. Review RUL trend report.
3. Check outlet temperature trend.
4. Check pressure ratio stability.
5. Schedule compressor inspection during the next maintenance window.
6. Follow safe shutdown procedure before physical inspection.
