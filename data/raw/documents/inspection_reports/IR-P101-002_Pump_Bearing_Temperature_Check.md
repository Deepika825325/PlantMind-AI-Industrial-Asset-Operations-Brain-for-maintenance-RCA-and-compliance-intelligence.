# IR-P101-002: Pump Bearing Temperature Check

Document ID: IR-P101-002
Asset ID: P-101
Asset Name: Cooling Water Circulation Pump
Asset Type: Pump
Document Type: Inspection Report
Inspection Date: 2026-01-22
Inspector: Demo Inspector
Inspection Status: Completed

---

## 1. Inspection Purpose

This inspection was performed to evaluate the bearing temperature condition of P-101 after high vibration and abnormal bearing noise were reported.

---

## 2. Inspection Scope

The inspection covered:

* Bearing housing temperature
* Bearing noise observation
* Lubrication evidence availability
* Need for corrective maintenance
* Risk of bearing wear

---

## 3. Measurement Summary

| Parameter           | Measured Value | Normal Limit | Warning Limit | Critical Limit | Status  |
| ------------------- | -------------: | -----------: | ------------: | -------------: | ------- |
| Bearing temperature |           78°C |   Below 65°C |          75°C |           80°C | Warning |

The bearing temperature is below the critical limit but close to the maximum allowed value.

---

## 4. Findings

* Bearing temperature was recorded at 78°C.
* Critical bearing temperature limit for P-101 is 80°C.
* Abnormal bearing noise was reported in the previous maintenance observation.
* Lubrication evidence for the current week was not available.
* High vibration was already reported in IR-P101-001.
* Bearing wear or lubrication degradation is suspected.

---

## 5. Risk Interpretation

P-101 requires maintenance attention because elevated bearing temperature, high vibration, abnormal bearing noise, and missing lubrication evidence are present together.

This combination may indicate:

* Lubrication degradation
* Bearing wear
* Shaft misalignment
* Excess friction in bearing housing
* Delayed lubrication inspection

---

## 6. Recommended Action

The maintenance team should:

1. Verify lubrication level.
2. Check lubricant quality.
3. Inspect bearing housing.
4. Review vibration inspection report IR-P101-001.
5. Check shaft alignment.
6. Create or update urgent maintenance work order.
7. Upload lubrication evidence after inspection.
